Summary

Premium CX is not “more agents.” It’s macro design, routing, evidence trails, QA calibration, and coaching loops—tied to scorecards that make quality visible.

1) Macro architecture that survives edge cases

A macro library isn’t a list—it’s a system. Your most common intents should map to templates with embedded policy logic, tone guidance, and evidence checklists. The goal: reduce cognitive load without sounding robotic.

Macro tiers

Tier 1 (simple), Tier 2 (policy), Tier 3 (exception handling).

Tone tokens

Small phrasing rules that keep voice consistent across agents.

2) QA rubrics that create consistency

QA should feel fair and actionable. Define a rubric that includes: correctness, tone, completeness, policy adherence, and resolution quality. Then calibrate weekly so scoring doesn’t drift.

Correctness
Did we do the right thing?
Tone
Does it sound like you?
Resolution
Was it truly resolved?

3) Escalation design (the hidden premium)

Escalations should be predictable: who owns what, when to escalate, and how we communicate internally. The best systems prevent escalations by giving agents decision power within guardrails.

Triggers

VIP, legal, safety, high-value orders, repeat incidents.

Ownership

Single-threaded owner with clear SLA and escalation ladder.

Evidence

Always include order context, policy, and customer history.

Pull quote
“Premium CX is a system, not staffing.”

Routing + macro architecture + QA calibration is what makes quality repeatable.

4) Coaching loops that stick

Coaching should be small, frequent, and tied to the rubric. Aim for 15-minute weekly sessions, a clear improvement goal, and a follow-up audit.

5) Weekly scorecards (WBR) that drive improvement

A WBR is not a slideshow. It’s a decision meeting with a scorecard, a short list of root causes, and a clear owner for each fix.

Template
  • Scorecard: SLA, QA, volume, backlog, CSAT
  • Top 3 friction points (with examples)
  • Root cause and fix proposal
  • Owners + due dates
  • Macro/SOP updates shipped this week
